name: Outreach cadence — 3-touch, Day 0/3/7
description: Standard sequence for cold outreach. Max 3 touches, escalating framing.
type: feedback
---

All cold outreach sequences are capped at **3 touches** with this cadence:
- **Touch 1 (Day 0):** Initial message — specific hook + teaser pitch + ask
- **Touch 2 (Day 3):** Bump with new info or softer angle — never "just bumping this up"
- **Touch 3 (Day 7):** Close-out — ask for redirect to the right person, or state you'll circle back next quarter

**Why:** Industry B2B norm is Day 0 / 3 / 7 — total ~10 days per sequence, which means a full batch cycles every ~2 weeks. Faster (0/2/5) feels aggressive on senior execs. Slower (0/5/12) loses momentum and signals lack of interest.

**How to apply:**
- When drafting a sequence, include all 3 touches up front (don't make the user ask for each follow-up)
- Set `Next Follow-up Due` in CRM:
  - After touch 1 → today + 3 days
  - After touch 2 → today + 4 days (which = Day 7 total)
  - After touch 3 → clear (move to Closed after no reply)
- First-90-days new-role execs can handle 0/2/5 (more openness, shorter window)
- 3 touches MAX. After that, move to Closed/Ghosted. Don't pad.

**Touch templates (adapt voice to your style memory):**
- **Touch 1:** Specific dated hook + 1-line pitch + soft ask ("worth 15 min?")
- **Touch 2:** New info or asset drop ("one-pager if useful:...") — never "just following up"
- **Touch 3:** Redirect ask ("if not the right team, can you point me at...") — if no redirect, circle back in Q+1
