Default cold outreach style is **teaser-short**, not information-heavy.

**The template:**
```
Hey {name}, we're working on something {company} could benefit from. Would love to tell you more.
```

Variations: "Worth 15 min?" / "Worth 15 min to share?" / "Would love to share more." / "Could I put 15 mins in your calendar?"

**Why:** Under ~100 chars reads like a busy operator typed it in 20 seconds. "Working on something" = curiosity hook without salesy specificity. Doesn't establish your identity or company in the message — your LinkedIn profile does that when they click through. No pitch = no objections for the prospect to generate. Low-pressure ask makes reply feel costless.

**How to apply:**
- Default to teaser style for all cold LinkedIn connection notes and DMs
- Save the detailed pitch for AFTER they reply asking for more
- Use specific dated hooks (their Q1 earnings comment, recent press quote, new-role announcement) only when the hook is very fresh (this week) — otherwise default teaser
- Email fallbacks (Day 7) can be slightly longer since email tolerates more length, but still lead teaser-first before the substance

**Follow-up after connection accept (post-accept DM style):**
Use observational/casual tone with a specific CTA. The working pattern:
```
Hey {name}, I can see you're no longer at {old co}, but I think you'll be interested in what we're building, could I put 15 mins in your calendar?
```

For someone who didn't change jobs, drop the "I can see you're no longer at..." clause:
```
Hey {name}, I think you'll be interested in what we're building. Could I put 15 mins in your calendar?
```

Key ingredients:
- "I can see you're..." — observational, references something from their profile
- "I think you'll be interested in what we're building" — curiosity pitch, not a feature list
- "could I put 15 mins in your calendar?" — specific CTA reads more human than bare "worth 15 min?"
- Comma splices are fine, slightly longer is human
- Don't over-trim — short-but-dry reads AI. Short-with-natural-flow reads human.

**Warm 1st-degree DM (they already know you OR you're already connected):**
Slightly more context OK, but still short. Reference their company specifically:
```
Hey {name}, we're working on something {company} could benefit from. Worth 15 min to share?
```

Or the credential form when you want to establish who you are:
```
Hey {name}, I run partnerships at {{YOUR_COMPANY}} ({{YOUR_COMPANY_ONE_LINER}}). {company}'s {specific_detail} is a natural fit. {{EXISTING_PARTNERS_REFERENCE}}. Worth 15 min?
```

**"Hey" > "Hi"** — feels warmer without being unprofessional. Always include a self-introduction ("I'm {{YOUR_NAME}}") on cold messages when prospects don't know you or your company.

**Not for:**
- Warm connections with prior context — those can reference specifics organically
- Email outreach with a dated hook (CNN quote, Q1 earnings mention) where the hook IS the message
